C-LOOK Disk Scheduling Algorithm

Overview

The C-LOOK algorithm is a variant of the LOOK scheduling algorithm. It moves the disk arm in one direction to service requests, and upon reaching the last request in that direction, it jumps back to the first request in the queue without servicing tracks in between. This behavior reduces unnecessary head movement.

This algorithm is especially efficient when dealing with requests that are clustered in specific regions of the disk.

Advantages

  • Minimizes total head movement compared to SCAN and LOOK.
  • Prevents starvation of requests.

Disadvantages

  • Complex implementation compared to simpler algorithms like FCFS.
  • Requests outside the servicing range may experience delays.
