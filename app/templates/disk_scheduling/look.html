LOOK Disk Scheduling Algorithm

Overview

The LOOK scheduling algorithm works similarly to the SCAN algorithm but avoids traversing to the extreme ends of the disk. The disk arm moves in one direction, servicing all requests in its path, and reverses direction once it reaches the last request in that direction. It is more efficient than SCAN in certain cases because it minimizes unnecessary head movements.

Advantages

  • Minimizes unnecessary head movements.
  • Reduces overall seek time compared to SCAN.

Disadvantages

  • May cause longer waiting times for requests just missed in one pass.
  • Complex implementation compared to FCFS.
